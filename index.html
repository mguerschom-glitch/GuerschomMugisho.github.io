Guerschom Mugisho

I am a Temporary Teaching and Research Assistant (ATER), currently completing my PhD in demography at Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne University. My thesis, supervised by Armelle Andro (Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne University) and Christophe Bergouignan (University of Bordeaux), examined the mechanisms through which conflict and conflict-related sexual violence affect fertility in the Great Lakes region.

I hold a Master’s degree in Demography (University of Strasbourg), a Master’s degree in Biostatistics and Health Econometrics (Aix-Marseille University), a University Diploma in Data Visualisation (University Institute of Technology of Paris – Rives de Seine), and a Bachelor’s degree in Economics (Université Évangélique en Afrique, DR Congo).

Affiliated with the Centre for Research in Demography at Paris 1 (CRIDUP), my research focuses on the effects of crises in a broad sense (conflicts, diseases – including epidemics and pandemics – and natural disasters) on fertility as well as on the health of women and children.
